Manchurian with Rice

 

Manchurian refers to a popular Indo-Chinese dish characterized by deep-fried vegetable or protein dumplings, such as cauliflower or paneer, coated in a tangy and spicy sauce. The crispy and flavorful dumplings are typically made with a batter of cornflour and spices, while the sauce includes ingredients like soy sauce, garlic, ginger, and green chilies. This delectable fusion dish exemplifies the harmonious blend of Chinese cooking techniques with Indian flavors, resulting in a savory and satisfying culinary experience. Manchurian is often served as an appetizer or paired with fried rice or noodles in many Asian restaurants around the world.
